Moguls

There were many people who impacted the movemtent in positive ways and I'm going to go over
the ones I belive had the most impact.

#1 N.W.A

This group consisted of five young men (Eazy-E,Dr. Dre,Ice Cube,MC Ren,DJ Yella) were some of the pionners in the 90s. They were from Compton, California, one of the worst parts of the country.
The group was notorious for using explicit words, they rose to fame after the release of "The Boyz n the Hood". Although this was in the late 80s they eventually they shaped 90s hip hop
with their music about women, drug dealing, gang activity, and police brutality which up until that point was never talked aobut in that manner.

#2 The Notorious B.I.G.

Christopher Wallace also known as The Notorious B.I.G or Biggie Smalls was a rpper from Brooklyn, New york and was one of the most influential rappers in te 90s. He is hailed as one of the greatest rappers of all time.
He gained popularity after signing to Bad Boy Entertainment and released "Party and Bushshit". He reached superstar status after he released his debut album Ready to Die in 1994 which help revive
the new york hip hop scene that was being over ran by west coast labels creating a rivalry. In 1997 while in Los Angeles Biggie was killed in a drive by shooting.
While having a short career he still had a masive influence in hip-hop to this day.

#3 Tupac Shakur

Tupac Shakur also known as 2 Pac was a rapper who grew up in Baltimore, Marland before moving to California in his teens where he got into the gang life ,in 1990 he joined an oakland based rap group and later released his solo debut album called 2Pacalypse Now. In 1994 he was arrested and served eight months in prison before being released on parole. He eventually signed with the infamous Death Row Records for the realease of his most famous album "All Eyes on Me". He had cemented him as not only one of the greatest artist of the 90s but of all time. In september of 1996 Shakur was leaving a Mike Tyson fight where he was shot, he died six days later leaving the hip hop community with a missing piece. 2 Tupac was one of the most influencial artist Hip Hop has ever seen. He was a true mogul to to the movement.
